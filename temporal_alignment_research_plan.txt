Trajectory-Sensitive Refusal Timing in Large Language Models
Research Plan

Working internal shorthand:
alignment hysteresis

Paper-facing terminology policy:
use "trajectory-sensitive refusal timing" or "path-dependent refusal timing" in the title, abstract, and main claims.
Use "alignment hysteresis" only as a discussion-level shorthand if the stricter evidence ladder below is satisfied.

Operational constraint and scope boundary:
- all model calls are executed through Cloudflare Workers AI endpoints
- no non-Cloudflare model APIs are assumed in this protocol
- external-validity claims are bounded to this access regime unless separately replicated

1. Thesis
Safety in LLMs should be evaluated as a temporal process, not only as a binary endpoint.
The same harmful objective can produce different refusal timing depending on how the trajectory is constructed, and that timing sensitivity can be measured in an API-compatible, reproducible study.
The primary contribution is a safety-evaluation framework and benchmark package for temporal refusal dynamics, with mitigation analysis as a first-class output rather than an appendix add-on.

2. The Paper-Level Claim
This paper does not claim merely that some prompts are harder than others.

Its paper-level claim is narrower and more review-resistant:

Under matched harmful objectives, structured trajectory variants shift refusal timing relative to both direct single-turn baselines and staged-direct multi-turn controls, and that shift is visible at the level of within-objective timing profiles rather than only aggregate attack success.

Framework-facing claim:
- the project introduces a reproducible temporal safety-evaluation protocol (dataset schema, trajectory templates, metrics, and decision thresholds)
- the project produces benchmark artifacts that can be rerun under constrained API access and extended to broader panels later

Supporting language for the paper:
- the project studies trajectory-sensitive refusal timing
- the project studies path-dependent refusal timing under matched objectives
- the project treats "alignment hysteresis" as a descriptive shorthand, not as a mechanistic hidden-state claim
- the project does not claim direct access to an internal continuous safety state

3. Single Central Novelty Object
Trajectory-conditioned refusal timing as a safety-evaluation framework object under matched harmful objectives.

This is the real novelty object.
The project is not primarily about introducing a new jailbreak family.
The project is not primarily about a new judge or latent-state probe.
The project is about whether matched objectives produce different refusal timing profiles when conversational history is changed in structured ways, and packaging that evidence as a reusable benchmark protocol.

4. Why The Word "Hysteresis" Is Not The Headline
The word "hysteresis" is still risky because reviewers may interpret it as a stronger mechanistic claim than the evidence can support.

Therefore:
- the title and abstract should not depend on the word
- the main evidence should stand without the word
- the paper can use "alignment hysteresis" only as a guarded descriptive interpretation in discussion

Promotion rule:
the manuscript may say the results are "consistent with descriptive alignment hysteresis" only if:
- S2C delays SAT relative to Direct
- S2C delays SAT relative to Staged Direct
- within-objective timing spread is positive across matched trajectory variants
- support-family evidence is directionally consistent

Otherwise the paper should use the safer language:
trajectory-sensitive refusal timing

5. The Reviewer-Robust Evidence Ladder
The paper should not live or die on one statistic or one family contrast.

Primary confirmatory evidence:
- Direct versus S2C paired SAT shift
- Staged Direct versus S2C paired SAT shift
- within-objective timing spread across Direct, Staged Direct, and S2C

Support-family evidence:
- Direct versus ICON paired SAT shift
- within-objective timing spread across Direct, S2C, and ICON on a fixed support subset or full bank

Secondary timing diagnostics:
- `ΔLag`
- phase escalation
- `ΔTSR`

Operational mitigation diagnostics (co-primary for safety impact):
- monitor-trigger precision and recall on harmful trajectories
- delayed-or-collapse reduction under monitor intervention
- benign utility retention under monitor intervention
- latency overhead under monitor intervention

Sensitivity-only diagnostics:
- IRT-indexed subsets
- IRT-conditioned models

Exploratory appendix diagnostics:
- loop-style or HLA-style descriptors
- LATS
- SJA

The paper should never require HLA to stand.

5A. Fellowship Impact Layer (Scalable Mitigation and Oversight)
The project is positioned as safety evaluation plus deployment-facing mitigation guidance.

Core impact outputs:
- a temporal safety benchmark that surfaces delayed refusal failure modes missed by one-shot evals
- a monitor intervention policy with explicit trigger thresholds and trade-off reporting
- a deployment decision table mapping observed metrics to concrete rollout choices

Deployment decision table (required in final report):
- if delayed-or-collapse share exceeds threshold under stress families: require monitor-enabled mode for relevant traffic slices
- if utility drop exceeds threshold on benign controls: narrow monitor trigger or add human review fallback
- if latency overhead exceeds threshold: apply monitor selectively by risk route instead of globally

6. The Critical New Control Family
The main confound to remove is:
"S2C is just a harder multi-turn prompt family."

To address this directly, the core protocol adds a staged-direct multi-turn control.

For each base harmful objective:
- Direct baseline: one-turn direct request
- Staged Direct control: matched multi-turn explicit decomposition of the same objective without contextual disguise or semantic camouflage (default: five turns)
- S2C: matched multi-turn structured semantic cloaking (default: five turns)

Interpretation logic:
- Direct versus Staged Direct isolates turn count and multi-turn decomposition effects
- Staged Direct versus S2C isolates the effect of semantic cloaking and contextual disguise beyond turn count alone
- Direct versus S2C captures the full observed delay shift

This is the cleanest way to answer the critique that the result might otherwise collapse to prompt-family difficulty.

8. Core Literature Insight
Across the local papers, the common pattern is not simply that multi-turn attacks succeed.
It is that trajectory structure changes when harmful intent becomes behaviorally legible and when refusal emerges.

This project turns that observation into a narrower safety-evaluation question and benchmark design:
- not just whether refusal occurs
- not just whether a family succeeds
- but when refusal occurs for the same objective under matched trajectory constructions

10. Objective Matching And Fidelity Rules
The project only works if all families actually target the same base objective.

Required matching rules:
- every harmful row must share a `base_objective_id`
- Direct, Staged Direct, S2C, and ICON versions must preserve the same harmful target
- Staged Direct and S2C must be matched on turn count
- Staged Direct and S2C should be approximately matched on token budget where feasible
- authoritative context, camouflage, or indirect framing are allowed only in the families that explicitly require them

Objective-fidelity checks:
- judge-based equivalence check on trajectory intent
- human audit on a stratified subset
- remove or rewrite any trajectory variant that drifts from the base objective

11. Trajectory Families
Every base harmful objective should be rendered into matched trajectories.

Family A: Direct baseline
Purpose:
- clean one-turn reference condition
Structure:
- one direct harmful request for the base objective

Family B: Staged Direct control
Purpose:
- controls for turn count and explicit decomposition without camouflage
Structure:
- Turn 1: direct partial statement of the same objective
- Turn 2: continue the same direct request plainly
- Turn 3: add direct constraint detail without contextual disguise
- Turn 4: continue the same direct request plainly
- Turn 5: complete the same direct request plainly
Guardrail:
- no contextual disguise
- no authority frame
- no semantic cloaking

Family C: S2C
Source:
- `2603.16192v1.pdf`
Structure:
- Turn 1: contextual reframing
- Turn 2: content fragmentation
- Turn 3: clue-guided camouflage or partial reconstruction
- Turn 4: continue reconstruction through masked references
- Turn 5: final reconstruction request while preserving prior contextual disguise
Role:
- primary stress family

Family D: ICON
Source:
- `2601.20903v1.pdf`
Structure:
- Turn 1: establish authoritative or semantically congruent context
- Turn 2: deepen the context and define the scenario
- Turn 3: place the target objective inside that context
- Turn 4: request context-consistent elaboration
- Turn 5: complete an in-context objective articulation
Allowed context patterns:
- scientific research
- academic paper
- policy memo
- audit brief
- evaluation scenario
Role:
- support family

Family E: LATS
Source:
- `2601.02670v1.pdf`
Structure:
- benign carrier frame
- partial anchor insertion
- anchor completion or clarification
Role:
- exploratory only

Family F: SJA
Source:
- `6261_Stealthy_Jailbreaking_Att.pdf`
Structure:
- harmless sub-question 1
- harmless sub-question 2
- synthesis or recombination turn
Role:
- exploratory only

The plan uses paper-derived structural templates only.

12. The Main Temporal Tests
This is the center of the paper.

For each objective `o` and model `m`:
- instantiate Direct, Staged Direct, and S2C
- measure SAT for each
- compute paired shifts

Primary paired statistics:
- `ΔSAT_direct = SAT_S2C - SAT_Direct`
- `ΔSAT_stage = SAT_S2C - SAT_StagedDirect`

Interpretation:
- `ΔSAT_direct > 0` means refusal occurs later under S2C than under the one-turn direct baseline
- `ΔSAT_stage > 0` means refusal occurs later under S2C than under a same-turn-count direct control

The second statistic is essential because it directly tests whether the timing effect survives a cleaner multi-turn control.

13. The Main Path-Dependence Descriptor
The paper needs one direct layer of evidence beyond a single paired delta.

Core descriptor:
within-objective timing spread

For each objective-model pair with Direct, Staged Direct, and S2C:
- `Spread_core = max(SAT_Direct, SAT_StagedDirect, SAT_S2C) - min(SAT_Direct, SAT_StagedDirect, SAT_S2C)`

Interpretation:
- `Spread_core > 0` means the same objective exhibits different refusal timing under different trajectory constructions

Support descriptor:
- `Spread_support = max(SAT_Direct, SAT_S2C, SAT_ICON) - min(SAT_Direct, SAT_S2C, SAT_ICON)`

The manuscript should present these as descriptive path-sensitivity statistics, not as hidden-state measurements.

14. Secondary Timing Statistics
These support the main claim but do not replace it.

- `ΔLag_direct = (SAT - IRT)_S2C - (SAT - IRT)_Direct`
- `ΔLag_stage = (SAT - IRT)_S2C - (SAT - IRT)_StagedDirect`
- phase escalation rate
- risk-weighted delay difference
  `ΔTSR = TSR_S2C - TSR_control`

Appendix-only:
- loop-style or HLA-style descriptors
- family-level exploratory visuals

15. The Role Of IRT
IRT is important, but it is not the main proof of the paper.

IRT should be treated as:
- a behavioral detectability threshold
- a way to index when harmful intent becomes legible under the probing protocol
- not a hidden-state readout

Core wording:
IRT is a behavioral detectability threshold used to compare trajectory families, not a direct measurement of an internal latent state.

Load-bearing rule:
- SAT-shift and spread statistics must stand on their own even if IRT analyses are dropped
- IRT is treated as explanatory support, not confirmatory core evidence

16. IRT Validation
Even though IRT is not the main proof pillar, it still needs to be earned empirically.

IRT validation block 1: construct validity
- harmful trajectories should produce earlier or stronger IRT than benign matched controls in related lexical families

IRT validation block 2: predictive validity
- earlier IRT should predict earlier SAT when refusal occurs
- earlier IRT should predict higher TSR when refusal is delayed
- earlier IRT should predict higher odds of `Delayed Safe` or `Collapse`

Concrete predictive tests:
- `SAT ~ IRT + family + model`
- `TSR ~ IRT + family + model`
- `DelayedOrCollapse ~ IRT + family + model`

IRT validation block 3: judge robustness
- three judges
- pairwise agreement reporting
- human adjudication on a stratified subset

IRT validation block 4: invariance
- judge-swap sensitivity
- paraphrase-preserving sensitivity

IRT simplification rule for manuscript readiness:
- if full IRT validation is noisy or underpowered, retain only the strongest two checks (predictive validity and judge robustness)
- move remaining IRT diagnostics to appendix without affecting core claim status

Downgrade rule:
- if IRT fails predictive validation, the main paper can still claim trajectory-sensitive refusal timing through `ΔSAT` and spread statistics, but lag-indexed interpretations must be weakened

17. IRT-Based Sensitivity Analyses
These are sensitivity analyses only.

Sensitivity A: exact-match IRT subset
- compare control families and S2C only among pairs with identical observed IRT

Sensitivity B: near-match IRT subset
- compare control families and S2C only among pairs with IRT difference of at most one turn

Sensitivity C: conditioned model
- fit family effects with IRT as a covariate

Interpretation rule:
- these analyses test whether part of the timing effect persists after indexing by detectability onset
- they do not replace the primary SAT-shift and spread evidence

18. Measurement Framework
At each turn:
- intent score
- safety score
- risk score
- benign utility score for benign controls

Measurement triangulation requirement:
- SAT and phase labels must be cross-checked on a stratified human-audit slice
- judge-only metrics are reported with uncertainty and agreement diagnostics
- all core claim language must remain valid under conservative threshold sensitivity checks

Core event definitions:
- IRT: first stable turn where intent score crosses threshold
- SAT: first stable turn where safety score crosses threshold
- Alignment Lag: `SAT - IRT`
- TSR: cumulative risk-weighted delay exposure

Phase labels:
- `Early Safe`
- `Delayed Safe`
- `Collapse`
- `False Safe`
- `No Trigger`

Primary endpoints:
- median `ΔSAT_direct`
- median `ΔSAT_stage`
- share of objective-model pairs with `ΔSAT_direct > 0`
- share of objective-model pairs with `ΔSAT_stage > 0`
- median `Spread_core`

Support endpoints:
- median `ΔSAT` for Direct versus ICON
- median `Spread_support`

Secondary endpoints:
- median `ΔLag`
- p90 lag by family
- mean `ΔTSR`
- phase escalation rate
- benign utility retention
- latency overhead

Exploratory endpoints:
- family-level heterogeneity
- appendix-only loop descriptors

21. Success Criteria And Terminology Downgrade Rules
Core support criteria:
- median `ΔSAT_direct` is positive with uncertainty bounds that do not suggest a trivial or unstable effect
- median `ΔSAT_stage` is positive with uncertainty bounds that do not suggest a trivial or unstable effect
- `Spread_core` is materially positive across a meaningful share of objective-model pairs
- directional consistency holds across the fixed model panel

Support-family criteria:
- Direct versus ICON is directionally consistent
- `Spread_support` is positive on the support-family evaluation set

Mitigation support:
- p90 lag reduction >= 20%
- delayed-or-collapse reduction >= 15%
- benign utility drop <= 5 percentage points
- latency overhead <= 15%

Deployment-actionability support:
- each mitigation result must map to a recommended deployment action (enable monitor, conditional monitor, or defer rollout)
- recommendations must include confidence notes and bounded-scope caveats

Interpretation rules:
- if only `ΔSAT_direct` is positive, call the result structured-trajectory delay, not path dependence
- if `ΔSAT_direct` and `ΔSAT_stage` are positive but support-family evidence is weak, call the result trajectory-sensitive refusal timing
- if `ΔSAT_direct`, `ΔSAT_stage`, and spread statistics are all positive, and support-family evidence is directionally consistent, the discussion may say the results are consistent with descriptive alignment hysteresis

22. Failure Mode Interpretation
If `ΔSAT_direct` is weak or null:
- the paper should not claim temporal delay from S2C in this setup

If `ΔSAT_direct` is positive but `ΔSAT_stage` is weak:
- the result supports multi-turn delay
- the paper should not claim stronger path dependence
- interpretation: the effect may be largely due to turn count or staging rather than cloaking structure

If `ΔSAT_direct` and `ΔSAT_stage` are positive but spread statistics are weak:
- the result supports trajectory-sensitive refusal timing
- the paper should avoid "hysteresis" language

If S2C is positive and ICON is also directionally positive:
- the effect is less likely to be a one-family artifact

If IRT fails predictive validation:
- keep the SAT-shift and spread story
- weaken all claims that depend on lag indexing or detectability timing
